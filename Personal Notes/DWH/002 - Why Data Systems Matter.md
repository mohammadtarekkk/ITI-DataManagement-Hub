# Why Data Systems Matter?

## The Problem: Data Silos
In many organizations, data is trapped in **Silos**—isolated systems that do not communicate with each other.

- **Vertical Fragmentation**: Departments (Sales, Marketing, HR) use separate systems.
- **Inconsistent Data**: "Customer ID" in CRM might be "Client No" in Billing, leading to mismatches.
- **Barriers**:
    - **Technical**: Different formats and lack of integration.
    - **Organizational**: Lack of cooperation and data sharing.

## The Solution: Integration
A Data Warehouse solves this by:
1. **Collecting**: Gathering data from heterogeneous sources.
2. **Integrating**: Standardizing formats and resolving inconsistencies (e.g., merging "Male/M/1" to "Male").
3. **Sharing**: Providing a unified view for cross-departmental analysis.

## Impact
Without a DWH, answering questions like *"How do marketing promotions affect inventory levels?"* requires manual, error-prone reconciliation of reports from different systems.
